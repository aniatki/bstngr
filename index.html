Shephard's Tone

Is an auditory illusion that creates the sensation of a tone that is forever ascending (or descending) in pitch, but never actually getting higher (or lower). It's like the musical version of a staircase that loops back on itself. It's made by layering tones that are separated by octaves, increase (or decrease) in pitch and are faded in and out smoothly as they move up/down. As one tone fades out at the top of the scale, another fades in at the bottom ‚Äî so your brain fills in the gap and hears a never-ending rise or fall.

In Dunkirk (2017), Zimmer used the Shepard's Tone to create a constant sense of rising tension. It feels like the music is always building ‚Äî anxiety, stress, urgency ‚Äî but never actually releases. The tone is layered into the background of ticking clocks and string instruments. It's especially noticeable in scenes like the Spitfire flying sequences or when the evacuation is in full swing. Even though the action may slow down or change focus, the tension never drops ‚Äî thanks to that illusion of eternal ascent.
